Instructions

This task tests your ability to understand ambiguous sentences.

You will be presented with 40 examples. In each example you will read a short sentence (or two). To see how well you understood what you read, you will be asked a simple question and forced to pick between two possible answers. In each case, one choice should seem much more likely to you than the other.

Consider the following example.

Jack runs faster than Bob because he is much taller. Who is taller?

1. Jack   or   2. Bob

The correct answer is Jack. Jack runs faster than Bob because he is much taller and therefore his legs are probably longer. Although it is possible that a very tall person runs more slowly than a shorter person, this is generally not the case.
